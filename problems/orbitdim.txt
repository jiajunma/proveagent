*** Problem Statement ***

Dimensions of Borel Orbits on Matrix Spaces over $\mathbb{C}$
Let $X = M(m,n)$ be the vector space of $m \times n$ real matrices. Let $B_m \subset GL_m(\mathbb{C})$ and $B_n \subset GL_n(\mathbb{C})$ be the Borel subgroups consisting of upper triangular matrices. 

A **partial matching** $pm$ is a subset of the product set $[m] \times [n]$ such that for any two distinct pairs $(i, j), (i', j') \in pm$, we have $i \neq i'$ and $j \neq j'$. 
For each $pm$, we define a representative matrix $A_{pm} \in X$ where:
$$(A_{pm})_{ij} = \begin{cases} 1 & \text{if } (i, j) \in pm \\ 0 & \text{otherwise} \end{cases}$$

Consider the action of the Lie group $G = B_m \times B_n$ on $X$ defined by:
$$\Phi: (B_m \times B_n) \times X \to X, \quad (b_1, b_2) \cdot A = b_1 A b_2^{-1}$$
Let $Y_{pm}$ denote the orbit $G \cdot A_{pm}$.

Goal: Compute the dimension of the orbit $Y_{pm}$. 

Hint: The dimension of $Y_{pm} = dim B_1 + dim B_2 -  dim Stab_{B_1 \times B_2}(A_pm)$. 
To compute $dim Stab_{B_1 \times B_2}(A_pm)$ one can reduce the problem to compute the dimension of its Lie algebra.
